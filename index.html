About Me

Hi, I am Yulia. I spent over 15 years working across internationally recognized high-impact research environments and applied biomedical settings, building a career at the intersection of molecular biology, data, and systems thinking. My work involved experimental design, complex data analysis, scientific publications, and interdisciplinary collaboration.

Over time, my responsibilities expanded beyond research into diagnostic and laboratory operational environments. During and post- COVID-19 pandemic, I contributed to laboratory testing efforts while also working at the intersection of laboratory logistics for a clinical study, equipment coordination, workflow optimization, and process coordination. Science increasingly became systems management — understanding how people, processes, data, and infrastructure interact under pressure.

At 37, I made a deliberate and, frankly, quite a courageous decision to transition into the data field — not as a reaction to circumstance, but as a direction I had already begun to pursue. My interest in data visualization started unexpectedly through watercolor, which led me to think more intentionally about structure, patterns, and clarity. That curiosity evolved into formal training: I enrolled in the Google Data Analytics program while still employed, long before anticipating a layoff.

When that transition eventually came, it felt less like disruption and more like alignment. I used the period as a focused investment phase — completing advanced coursework, an intensive Data Analytics bootcamp, and building applied projects. What might have been a pause became acceleration.

I genuinely enjoy digging into messy, imperfect data and doing what I call “forensic EDA” — a structured, investigative approach to exploratory analysis. For me, analysis begins long before dashboards or models. It starts with questioning the integrity of the data itself: cleaning inconsistencies, validating assumptions, identifying structural anomalies, tracing duplicates, and resolving cross-field conflicts. I approach datasets the way I once approached experiments in the lab — carefully, skeptically, and methodically — until the underlying logic becomes clear. Turning chaos into structured clarity is not just a task; it is the part of the process I enjoy most.

Today, I focus on Data Analytics and Business Intelligence, working with SQL, Python, R, and modern visualization tools to transform complex datasets into clear, reliable insights. I build analytical datasets, define validation rules, design dashboards, and translate numbers into narratives that decision-makers can trust. My goal is not just to produce metrics, but to create analytical frameworks that are transparent, reproducible, and aligned with real business questions. Whether exploring operational systems, performance metrics, or complex multi-source datasets, I aim to combine technical rigor with clarity — delivering insights that are both technically sound and strategically meaningful.

Projects

Portugal Property Atlas

March 2026

Python Google Colab Power BI

A thorough exploration of the Portuguese real estate market based on web-scraped property listings. The project goes beyond standard exploratory analysis by applying a forensic EDA approach — systematically investigating data inconsistencies, structural anomalies, duplicate listings, and cross-field conflicts. Special attention is given to property-type context, recognizing that apartments, houses, land, and commercial assets follow fundamentally different size and reporting conventions. By reconstructing reliable area metrics and validating relationships within each category, the analysis transforms noisy listing data into a structured, analytically sound dataset suitable for modeling and market insights.

  • Identified and resolved structural inconsistencies across property types, reconstructing reliable area metrics
  • Designed validation rules to detect cross-field conflicts and quantified anomaly prevalence
  • Built a cleaned, modeling-ready dataset and documented all transformation logic for reproducibility
